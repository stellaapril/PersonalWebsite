WEATHER FORCASTING

DATA ANALYTICS | R

INTRODUCTION

Every February 2nd, a crowd of thousands gathers at Punxsutawney, Pennsylvania, to await a special forecast from a groundhog named Phil. Whether Phil has a forecasting ability is a century old debate.

Based on the analysis of average temperatures of February and March, and snowfall data, we can predict whether February has entered Early Spring. Compared the determination results through the temperatures data and the results through snowfall data with Phil’s forecasts, and we can evaluate whether Phil has a real forecasting power, and we will know did Phil’s accuracy rate and did Phil’s forecast even worse than random chance?

METHODOLOGY

TEMPERATURE METHODOLOGY

Compared Average of 1901-2000 February Temperature with each year February Average Temperature, if the single year’s Feb average temperature is lower than Average of Century years February Temperature, it marked “Below”, otherwise, it marked “Above”. Compared Average of 1901-2000 March Temperature with each year March Average Temperature, if the single year’s Mar average temperature is lower than Average of Century years Mar Temperature, it marked “Below”, otherwise, it marked “Above”.

  • When both Feb and Mar are above the century average temperature, we predict Early Spring coming.
  • On the other hand, when both of them is below the century average temperature, we predict it still Winter.
  • When Feb is below, but Mar is above, it indicated the temperature is gradually increasing, so we predict Early Spring is coming.
  • When Feb is above but Mar is below, it indicated the temperature is decreasing in the March, we predict it still Winter. Unless, if that year the Feb Average is higher than March, we can predict Early Spring is coming.

Determine Logical:

Feb Above & Mar Above Feb Above & Mar Below Feb Below & Mar Above Feb Below & Mar Below
Early Spring Feb Avg Temp < Mar Avg Temp : Early Spring
Feb Avg Temp < Mar Avg Temp : Winter 		Early Spring Winter

METHODOLOGY

SNOWFALL METHODOLOGY

Compared Average of Century Years February Snowfall with each year February Average Snowfall, if the single year’s Feb average temperature is lower than Average of Century Years February Snowfall it marked “Below”, otherwise, it marked “Above”. Compared Average of Century Years March Snowfall with each year March Average Snowfall, if the single year’s Mar average Snowfall is lower than Average of Century Years March Snowfall, it marked “Below”, otherwise, it marked “Above”.

If snow continually melts as it lands, and the accumulation never reaches 0.1 inches on your measuring surface, snowfall should be recorded as a trace (T) and record in your remarks that the "snow melted as it landed". So, when the value is T, it below the Average.

When Both Feb & Mar snowfall are Above Average Snowfall, it predict as Winter, otherwise it will be Spring.

Determine Logical:

Feb Above & Mar Above Feb Above & Mar Below Feb Below & Mar Above Feb Below & Mar Below
Winter Early Spring Early Spring Early Spring

HYPOTHESIS TESTING

T.Test(Phil’s Accuracy-temperature methodology, Random Chance 0.5):

NULL Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy is equal to Random Chance(0.5)

Alternative Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy is less than Random Chance(0.5)

Result: The p-value is 1.771e-08, it is <0.05, which means Phil’s prediction accuracy and Random Chance has a statistically significant difference. So, we have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis. The 95 percent confidence interval is (-Inf,0.33). The mean of Phil’s prediction accuracy is 0.26.

Proportions Test:

Phil’s prediction accuracy(Temperature Methodology)and random chance (0.5)

Result: The p-value is 11.64e-07, it is <0.05, which means Phil’s prediction accuracy and Random Chance has a statistically significant difference. So, we have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis.

Proportions Test:

Phil’s prediction accuracy(Snowfalls Methodology) and random chance (0.5)

NULL Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy (Snowfalls Methodology) is equal to Random Chance(0.5)

Alternative Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy (Snowfalls Methodology) is less than Random Chance(0.5)

Result: The p-value is 2.2e-16, it is <0.05, which means Phil’s prediction accuracy (Snowfalls Methodology) and Random Chance has a statistically significant difference. So, we have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis.

Result of Phil’s Forecasting Accuracy Result:

T.Test(Phil’s Accuracy-temperature methodology, Phil’s Accuracy-snowfalls methodology):

NULL Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy result(temperature methodology) is equal to Phil’s Accuracy result(Denver snowfall methodology)

Alternative Hypothesis: Phil’s prediction accuracy result(temperature methodology) is not equal to Phil’s Accuracy result(Denver snowfall methodology)

Result: The p-value is0.00102, it is <0.05, which means Phil’s prediction accuracy result(temperature methodology) and Phil’s Accuracy result(Denver snowfall methodology) has a statistically significant difference. So, we have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis.

The 95 percent confidence interval is (0.06691798 ,0.26066822)

The mean of Phil’s prediction accuracy result(temperature methodology) : 0.258

The mean of Phil’s Accuracy result(Denver snowfall methodology) : 0.094

These two different methods results of Phil’s accuracy are different.

CONCLUSIONS

The temperature methodology Phil’s prediction accuracy is 25.8%, and the Snowfalls methodology Phil’s predication accuracy is 9.5%. The 4.1&4.2 hypothesis testing indicates that Phil has no predication power, and accuracy less than random chance. In addition, the Phil’s accuracy determined by two different methodology, their results have a statistically significant different.
