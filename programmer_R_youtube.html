TRENDING YOUTUBE VIDEO USER INTERACTIONS

DATA ANALYTICS | R

INTRODUCTION

Backgrounds:

YouTube is an American online video-sharing platform headquartered in San Bruno, California. According to Variety magazine, “To determine the year’s top-trending videos, YouTube uses a combination of factors including measuring users’ interactions (number of views, shares, comments and likes). Note that they’re not the most-viewed videos overall for the calendar year”.

YouTube has gradually become popular in these years. Users can use lots of tools to express their perspective or attitudes to each video. Like button, dislike button and comments box are tools to measure how the content is received by the public at large. It indicates the engagement on a video because people choose to like, dislike or write a comment a video when they can relate to it at a personal level, even if they disagree.

Base on the analysis of number of likes/dislikes, comments and views of YouTube video, we can study the different user interactions in different countries. We can evaluate whether higher number of views has a higher likes rate or comments rate.

Questions:

Question1: Are Users from different countries have different user interaction?

Question2: Is there have any correlation between like rate (dislike rate / comments rate) and number of views?

Question3: Whether videos with a higher user interaction have a higher number of views?

METHODOLOGY

Dataset:

Trending YouTube Video Statistics DataSet from Karggle

USvideos.csv

Jpvideos.csv

Variables:

The Likes Rate = number of likes / number of views

The Dislikes Rate = number of dislikes / number of views

The Comments Rate = number of comments/ number of views

Interaction Score = Comments Rate * Likes Rate * Dislikes Rate

High Interaction Level = Comments Rate * Likes Rate * Dislikes Rate >= Median(Comments Rate * Likes Rate * Dislikes Rate)

Low Interaction Level = Comments Rate * Likes Rate * Dislikes Rate < Median(Comments Rate * Likes Rate * Dislikes Rate)

Clean up Dataset:

Clean up the dataset to remove all rows which (comments_disabled) or (ratings_disabled) or (video_error_or_removed) is TRUE before analysis

Because length of two datasets are different. I sort and select Top 10000 Views of Videos in both US and Japan. Using the t-test to compare their user interaction trough rate of likes, rate of dislikes, rate of comments.

Remove outlier data for user interaction level boxplot, remove data lower than 5%, and higher than 95%.

HYPOTHESIS TESTING

T.Test(Likes Rate in U.S , Likes Rate in Japan):

NULL Hypothesis: The Likes Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is equal to the Likes Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

Alternative Hypothesis: The Likes Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is not equal to the Likes Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

Result: The p-value is 2.2e-16, it is <0.05, which means The Likes Rate of YouTube Trending Videos in U.S area is not equal to the Likes Rate in Japan area. They have a significant difference. We have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis. The mean of Likes Rate in U.S is 3.49%, and the mean of Likes Rate in Japan is 2.11%. So, we know that users in U.S and Japan have a different ratio of using likes button after viewing the video on YouTube.

T.Test(Dislikes Rate in U.S , Dislikes Rate in Japan):

NULL Hypothesis: The Dislikes Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is equal to the Dislikes Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

Alternative Hypothesis: The Dislikes Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is greater than the Dislikes Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

Result: The p-value is 0.9567, it is >0.05, which means We have 95.67% chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis. We cannot conclude that a significant difference exists. They did not have a significant difference. So, we retain the NULL Hypothesis and reject the alternative hypothesis. The Dislikes Rate of YouTube Trending Videos in U.S area is not greater than the Dislikes Rate in Japan area.

T.Test(Comments Rate in U.S , Comments Rate in Japan):

NULL Hypothesis: The Comments Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is equal to the Comments Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

Alternative Hypothesis: The Dislikes Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is not equal to the Comments Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos

The p-value is 0.002062, it is <0.05, which means The Comments Rate in U.S YouTube Trending Videos is not equal to the Comments Rate in Japan YouTube Trending Videos. They have a significant difference. We have a low chance to incorrectly reject the NULL Hypothesis. The mean of Comments Rate in U.S is 0.39%, and the mean of Comments Rate in Japan is 0.37%. So, we know that users in U.S and Japan have a different ratio of comments after viewing the video on YouTube.

CONCLUSIONS

In different countries, users have different user interaction. Users in U.S and Japan have a different ratio of using likes button or ratio of writing comments after viewing a trending video on YouTube. However, they have a same ratio of using dislikes button. There is no significant correlation between interaction score and number of views. Thus, the increasing of number of views will not increase the user interactive level in both US and Japan.
